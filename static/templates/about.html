Game Theory

It's subtle, but it's important:

On the one hand
If you are only allowed to vote for a single candidate, then similar candidates split votes and lose elections. Voters are left with extreme candidates. Any more than 2 candidates and the centrists have little chance of winning because the current voting system forces similar candidates to sabotage each other. Elections quickly reduce to 2 candidates who tend to polarize voters, empowering 2 political parties to control their candidates. Campaigns use negative ads because they have to convince voters that they deserve a vote and their opponent does not.
On the other hand
If you are allowed to vote for any of the candidates, then you can vote for a third-party candidate without wasting your vote. You can still vote for a major party candidate as well. More candidates can enter a race without distorting the votes for the others. Elections can include 3 or more candidates without creating a disadvantage for the centrists. Politicians are empowered relative to their political parties. The goal of campaigning becomes convincing voters that you deserve their approval, with less incentive for negative campaigning.



What's the big deal?

Radicalized politics has made the political parties into teams that play to win at the expense of the nation.

At the root of this problem is the structure of our voting system.

When citizens may only approve of a single candidate in an election, powerful unhealthy incentives can have an influence on candidates, campaigns, the media, and society.

Third-party candidates have little chance of winning, and end up hurting their most similar mainstream candidate.

By simply allowing voters to vote for as many of the candidates as they like, the incentives change and we get better elections.
