Our choice of voting systems gives us bad outcomes. With subtle changes to how we vote, new incentives will lead to better politics, better elections, and a stronger American democracy.

We are demonstrating "Approval Voting" so that the public can experience the superior expression of their preferences as they vote:

  • Incentives for negative campaigning are transformed into incentives for candidates seeking approval.
  • A vote for a third-party candidate is not a wasted vote.
  • Power is shifted toward the candidates and away from their political parties.


Game Theory

It's subtle, but it's important:

On the one hand
If you are only allowed to vote for a single candidate, then centrists and similar candidates split votes and lose elections. With more than 2 candidates the centrists spilit votes and have little chance of winning. Elections quickly reduce to 2 candidates who tend to polarize voters, empowering 2 political parties to control their candidates. Campaigns use negative ads because they have to convince voters that they deserve a vote and their opponent does not.
On the other hand
If you are allowed to vote for any number of candidates, then you can vote for a third-party candidate without wasting your vote. You can still vote for a major party candidate as well. More candidates can enter a race without distorting the votes for the others. Elections can include 3 or more candidates without creating a disadvantage for the centrists. Politicians are empowered relative to their political parties. The goal of campaigning becomes convincing voters that you deserve their approval, with less incentive for negative campaigning.


What's the big deal?

Radicalized politics has made the political parties into teams that play to win at the expense of the nation.

At the root of this problem is the structure of our voting system.

When citizens may only approve of a single candidate in an election, powerful unhealthy incentives can have an influence on candidates, campaigns, the media, and society.

Third-party candidates have little chance of winning, and end up hurting their most similar mainstream candidate.

By simply allowing voters to vote for as many of the candidates as they like, the incentives change and we get better elections.
